One of the many groups from the Cordillera that has truly kept its culture as a way of life is the Tingguian group.

Abra is known for its Pottery.

Posted by Start Bootstrap on August 24, 2014

Abra is bounded on the north by Ilocos Norte and Apayao, on the South by Mountain Province and Ilocos Sur, and on the east by Kalinga. Geographically,Abra, with a total land are of 397.8 thousand hectares, is composed of highlands and lowlands. Tineg, Tubo, Malicbong, and Licuan-Baay belong to the highland areas inhabited by the Tingguians. Other areas occupied by the Tingguians are Binley, Bucloc, Daguiman, Danglas, Lacub, Langilang, Lagayan, Langiden, Luba, Manabo, Sallapadan, San Juan, San Quintin, Penarrubia and Villaviciosa.

The word "Tingguian" was derived from the word tingue which means "Mountaineers. Tingguian,therfore, refers to the "people of the mountains".Itneg is what the Tingguians are called in Ilocano Dialect

Today, there are two identifiable Tingguian groups, namely the "valley Tingguian" and the "mountain Tingguian". The former occupy the village communities where there are also Ilocano settlers, While the latter are distributed in sparsely populated areas in the highland country of northern and eastern Abra.

The Traditional Attires of Abra

A distinct characteristic of Tingguians is their predilication for beauty and simplicity.

The Tingguian woman are extremely fond of beads, and are particularly noted for their creative designs in weaving, bead making, basketry, and pottery. They weave their cloth from locally produced materials. The wrap-around skirt is called dinwa. The white dinwa with re embroidere boarder is called piningitan, while the dark blue colored one with geometric patterns is called kinamayan. The upper garment used maybe any shirt similar to kimona, a modern blouse design developed to match the design of the tapis. The belft is called called balikes measuring around 2 yards long. A more allaborate belt is called anigtan. They also wear beads called tinali that may extend up to the upper arms, and removal of this usually reveals tattoo.

For Tingguian men the lowergarment is called ba-al; it is made of woven cloth of predeominantly white color. Durind special occasions, an upper garment called bado is worn. It is a long-sleved jacket which goes with a belt into which a long working knife is sheathed. Ayabong is the headdress worn by men.

Arya Abra

The Arya! Abra Festival is a week-long celebation that coincides with the founding anniversary of the province of Abra. This is usually celebrated from March 6-10. Arya ! Abra means "Onward Abra" the whole festival is aimed at creating greater ppreciation for the Abareno Culture.

Province Of Abra Official Seal

Tourists from around the Philippines and abroad are invited to discover Abra's treasures that include, among others, the unspoiled wonders of nature, rich culture, unique arts and crafts, exotic cuisine and their traditional hospitality.

Every year, Arya! Abra adopts theme that serves as the basis for all activities to be conducted. In order to promote the products of the province, all the towns participated in a trade fair, showcasing their own specialties. The festival included a Youth day with a parade and strret dancing, highlighted by a Mascot competition, International Women's Day celebration, an acrobatic show, and the championship game of the Congressional Cup.
