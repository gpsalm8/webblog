Headhunters and Warriors

The name Kalinga is belived to have come from the Ibanags(indegenous group living along the banks of the Cagayan river) and the Gaddangs(indegenous group of Nueva Viscaya, Isabela, Quirino, and Cagayan). The name "Kalinnga" is believed to have been derived from the Ibanag word "kalinga and the Gadang word "kalinga", both meaning headhunters

Kalinga is bounded on the north by Apayao, on the south by Cagayan Valley, on the west by Abra, and on the sout by mountain Province. It has a total land area 311,970 hectares.

The Traditional Attires of Kalinga

The Kalinga wrap-arund skirt is called ka-in which is embroidered and embedded with shells called chawisak or wituwitan The headdress is called appong, sipor or apungot. The beads called umayway a kok are worn diagonally accross the chestor on one side. This is called winayway or kokko-on.

The males wear G-string called ba-ar or fa-ar, with tattoos and other body ornaments. The tattoo designs proclaims a man's bravery during tribal war. The feather headdress is called duruw. The males wear necklaces called bungor.

The Kalinga Festival

Ullalim Festival

The province of Kalinga has always emphasized the beauty and uniqueness of its unblemished culture, and more so through the annually celebrated Ullalim Festival which began in 1995.

Province Of Kalinga Official Seal

The festival presents the different cultural dances of the Kalingas. Indigenous bamboo musical insturments are used, and the main musial instrument is the gong.

The actiivities further lend legitimacy to the Kalinga culture that has somehow managed to escape the harsh influence of foreign rulers.

Whether on stage, at the dinig table, or in the playing field, the Kalinga culture remains intact and unique, after hundreds of years.
