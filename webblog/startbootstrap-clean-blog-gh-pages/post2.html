Apayao is a landlocked placed bounded on the north and east by Cagayan, on the south by Kalinga, and on the west by Ilocos Norte. It has a total land area of 5113 sq km.

The word "Tingguian" was derived from the word tingue which means "Mountaineers. Tingguian,therfore, refers to the "people of the mountains".Itneg is what the Tingguians are called in Ilocano Dialect

Today, there are two identifiable Tingguian groups, namely the "valley Tingguian" and the "mountain Tingguian". The former occupy the village communities where there are also Ilocano settlers, While the latter are distributed in sparsely populated areas in the highland country of northern and eastern Abra.

The Traditional Attires of Apayao

The Apayao wrap-around skirt is called aken which usually comes in a rich blue color with red embroidery called armat or dinang-dangran. It goes with a belt girdle called bahakat. The upper garment is called badiyu, a short-waisted, long leeved pull-on shirt which is usually black, navy blue or red. Apayao female accessories include sapahaw, a headband ecorated with beads; laddong head cloth decorated with beads and spangles; pawisak or lubayearrings; ambusaw, a necklace with larger beads; patti, a red, blue or green that is hung around the head decoratting the laddong; pasag, small beads for the ankles; sinali, a bracelet; and sangkilat, a silver ring.

The Apayao G-string ba-ag is broader than most of the G-strings of other groupss. Predominantly blue in color and tassle at the ends. An embroidered jacket is worn during special occasions. The headdress used by men is called abungot. The necklace is called sipital, and it is made of shells shaped into two-lobed clover shapes called pawisak.

Say-am Apayao

The Say-am Apayao is the grandest of all the celebrations held in Apayao. It showcases the ethnic songs and dance rituals of the Isnags. Other festivals of Apayao are the following: Apayao day February 14; Conner, every third week of May; and the Pudtol Town fiesta, every last Thursday, Friday and Saturday of May.

The customs and traditions of the Isnags are interwoven in their daily activities, festivities, and celebrations. The Isnags practice rituals and customs that correspond to every activity such as farming (aga-alikam), the death anniversary of a relative (abobat), marriage (aggaatawa) and feasting (Say-am). These practices have been modified through the years with the inculcation of Christianity, and with the influx of immigrants from other provinces who brought with them their own cultures.

Every year, Arya! Abra adopts theme that serves as the basis for all activities to be conducted. In order to promote the products of the province, all the towns participated in a trade fair, showcasing their own specialties. The festival included a Youth day with a parade and strret dancing, highlighted by a Mascot competition, International Women's Day celebration, an acrobatic show, and the championship game of the Congressional Cup.
