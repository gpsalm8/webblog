Ifugao is located in the southern part of the Cordillera region, is best known for its famous rice terraces of Banaue in modern times have become one tourrist actractions of the Philippines. It is bound on the North by Mountain Province, on the south by Nueva Vizcaya, on the east by Isabela, and on the west by Benguet. Ifugao is separated from Botoc, mountain Province and Benguet by the Mount Polis range, while on its southwest stands Mount Pulag. It is characterized by rugged mountains, great forests and rivers, and gently rolling hills and plateaus in the east and south.

Aside from their terraces the Ifugaos are known for their literary traditins of the 'Hudhud' and the 'Alim'. The term Ifugao is derived from the word ipugo which means "earth people" or "mortals" or "humans" as distinguish from spirits and deities. It also means "from the hills" as pugo means "hill". The Ifugaos highest prestige are the hagabi, for the wealthiest; and the uyauy, a feast for those immediately below the wealthiest.

The Traditional Attires of Ifugao

The Ifugao wrap-arund skirt torgeh/tolge or ampuyo and is predominantly black, red, white and yellow in color. The ingalgaletget tapis is a two-paneled skirt locally produced in Kiangan and worn by worker women. The upper garment is called lamma. It is used by working women to protect their backs from the sun and the weeds, during weedings, and harvesting.

The Ifugao Festival

Imnbayah and Urpihad Festival of Banaue

Imbayah is a rite of prestige performed by the elite of banaue to announce their ascendency to village aristocracy. Urpih on the other hand, is a thanksgiving festival to celebrate the planting of the rice in the fields. Both festivities are marked with merrymaking, like rice wine drinking, chanting and feasting on sacrificed animals.

Province Of Ifugao Official Seal

The Municipality of Banaue alternately celebrates these two Ifugao festivities during the third week of April. The three-day cultural festival highlights ethnic games, sports and traditional crafts. Various competitions are held, including gong-playing, arm wrestling, and leg wrestling for both adults and youths

One of the Crowds favorites in the Imbayah' Festival is the homemade wooden scooter race from Banaue oint running downhill for three miles to Plaza.
