In the southernmost part of the Cordillera region lies the province of Benguet. It covers an area of 2,616 square kilometers spread nearly entirely over the Cordillera Central eastward toward the line of peaks and ridges of the Polis Range and southeastward into theCaraballo Mountains. The Major rivers of luzon, the Abra, he Agno, and the Amburayan, have their headwaters in Benguet. The third highest Mountain in the Philippines - Mount Pulag - is in Benguet. The Ibalois have that it is a place where their spirits go to rejoin their ancestors.

Several ethnolinguistic groups people Benguet, namely, the Ibalois, Kankana-eys,Kalanguyas or Ikalahans, Karaos and Iwak/Iowak make up the 330,000 total population of the province.

The Traditional Attires of Benguet

The Benguet wrap-arund skirt devi/divit/eten and the blouse kambal or sa-dey both display broad horizontal bands of different colors, with white, dark blue, and red predominating. The kambal has several layers of plaid at the back, purposely to proytect the user for the edges of the kayabang basket which is carried at the back.

Colors signify the status of the user. Black and white is said to be foer women of ordinary status; while women who belong to wealthy families wear more colorful kambal and eten, like the kambayasho with different color combinations of green and yellow.

The women also wear headbands called pangjet which is usually of the same material as the devit and kambal, and bead necklaces, made of the seeds of abukay,takdian, or kataynan, a reed plant, and bronze bracelets called karing.

The Ordinary G-string of the Ibaloi and Karao is called pinangsas or kulibao/kolebaw kuval/kobal. The head cloth or headdress is either called shenget, pangjet, or binatjek, balangosong, or salibubo. It is used by men and the mambonong or high priest.

The Adivay Festival

The word adivay, a local term meaning "gathering of all indigenous people in celebration and thanksgivimg", became the theme to celebrate Benguet's founding anniversary. It began in 2005 and it is one of the festivals fuly supported by the Department of Tourism, promoting food, dances, songs, culture and the Benguet people themeselves.

Highlighted during the event are agri-industrial activities, farmer interaction, and information exchanges, fashion shows, market encounters, technology forums and tourism activities like kayaking, mountain hiking, and trekking, food discovery challenges, and cook-fests.

The most exciting part of the festival is the showdown among Benguet's 13 municipalities. The finest products from each town, including indigenous artifacts, native delicacies and picturesque sites in miniature models are presented in a country fair..
