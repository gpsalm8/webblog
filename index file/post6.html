The old Cordillera Region was called Mountain Province and its capital. This province is bounded in the north by Abra and kalinga, in the south by Ifugao, in the west by Ilocos Sur, and in the east by Isabela. Ome parts of the Mt. Province is so rugged and have high elevations that frost has been known to occur. The Spaniards were unable to establish administrative presence here until 1850. Hence, the native culture was largely undisturbed for some time.

The population in this mountainous area is predominantly populated by two ethnolinguistic groups namely, the Bontocs and the Kankana-eys.

The Traditional Attires of Mountain Province

The bontocs wear the tapis called tergey/lufid or pitay. A thick woven belt called wakes,bakget or falikos is use to hold the lufid in place. In olden days, th women did not wear any upper garments. Today, the womenwear blouses called lamma.

The males wear G-string called wakes or wanes/ba-ag. It partly covers the buttocks and the groin. The men's headdress is called pallaka in bontoc

The Mountain Province Festival

Lang-ay Festival

Mountani Province's Lang-ay Festival is now being hailed as the biggest atraction every year in April. Born during the 30th anniversary of MOuntain Province, the Lang-ay Festival was the brainchild of provincial Leaders is a bid to call the attention to the province's rich and diverse culture as well as the peoples pride for their god given wonders and gifts.
